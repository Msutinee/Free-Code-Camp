Early life

Gabrielle Bonheur Chanel was born on the 19th of August, 1883 in Saumar, France. When Gabrielle was 12 her mother died of Bronchitis, her brothers were sent to work on a farm and her and her two sisters were sent to an orphanage.

Early Career

In the orphanage she learned how to sew. When she was 18 she started singing in clubs and changed her name to Coco Chanel. There she met a millionaire called Étienne Balsan. She then became his mistress and lived with him in his chateau Royallieu in Paris. She then had an affair with Balsan's friend, Captain Arthur Edward Capel.

1910-1912

Chanel began designing hats while living with Balsan. She became a licensed milliner in 1910 and opened a boutique at 21 rue Cambon, Paris named Chanel Modes. As this location already housed an established clothing business, Chanel sold only her millinery creations at this address. Chanel's millinery career bloomed once theatre actress Gabrielle Dorziat modelled her hats.

1913-1920's

Chanel opens a new boutique in Deauville, France and introduces a collection of sports wear.In 1915 Chanel opened her first couture house in Biarritz, France. She opened another couture house in 1918. In 1921 Chanel's first perfume Chanel no.5 was launched. In the 1920's Chanel introduced the little black dress and then in 1925 she introduced the Chanel suit. In 1924 she introduced the creation of her first line of makeup. In 1924-1928 Coco Chanel worked as a designer for Russian seasons

1930's

Despite her great success, Chanel closed the doors of her salon in 1939, when France declared war on Germany. Other couturiers left the country, but Chanel endured the war in Paris, her future uncertain. Chanel found she could not idly stand by and observe the early success of Christian Dior whose "New Look" prevailed in the postwar period. Chanel set out to rescue and reinvigorate women's fashion.

1950's

Chanel opens a new boutique in Deauville, France and introduces a collection of sports wear.In 1915 Chanel opened her first couture house in Biarritz, France. She opened another couture house in 1918. In 1921 Chanel's first perfume Chanel no.5 was launched.

1970-1985's

Chanel died on January 10th, 1971 at the Hotel Ritz, where she lived for more than 30 years. Following Chanel's death in 1971, several of her assistants designed the couture and ready-to-wear lines until Karl Lagerfeld took over the design in 1983 and ready to wear in 1984. Lagerfeld, like Chanel, looked to past designs for the secret to his success. His designs incorporated signature Chanel details, tweed fabrics, colours, gold chains, quilt-stitched leather and the linked "CC" logo.
